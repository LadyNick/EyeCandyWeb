What happened while taking CS179N at UCR?

For CS179N at UCR, we were instructed to split the plans for our game into 100 points with different priority levels. We had 7 puzzles, each with 10 points, totaling 70 points. The remaining 30 points accounted for the introduction, the dialogue, characters, settings, and things of that matter. Each team member would be working on something every week, and something or even multiple things were supposed to be getting done every week. Unfortunately that wasn't always the case in my group, and I completed did points of the game. For others, it was taking 6 or 7 weeks to have a total of 10 points completed, and even then there was a lack of effort in some areas or it looked unfinished. For the last 3 weeks, for everyone to get up to 20 points, because each member was supposed to do 20 points each, the remaining work was rushed, and not up to par. I ended up completing a third of the game. There were multiple points that were awarded to a member as complete, even though they weren't, or we're completed by another member.

I am the type of person to not release or present anything unless I am very happy with it. I had a vision for this game, and it wasn't carried out. My plan is to scrap and then rebuild most of the game, adding changes where I see needed. I plan to release Eye Candy Bakery solo on steam before the Spring Season of 2024.

For the original proposal, scrum sheet, and files, go to the bottom of the page.

What are the planned changes?

Introduction

For the introduction, for the most part everything is to my liking because I made all the sprites animations. I originally created a customizable player and name menu accounting for hair, skin, and shirt(eye) colors, but I have decided that I would like to add an actual eye customizable color. Originally the player had black eyes by default but I'm going to change it so the eyes can look like Candy's and Coco's. This means I will be creating more sprites and more animations.

Setting

The acutal outdoors art and characters are to my liking since I did it myself during the course. However, when the player goes into the bakery, it simply goes to the bakery background with the two larger Candy and Coco sprites present. I want to add a room area for the player to be able to walk around inside of the bakery, going to certain spots to do different steps of the baking process. I think this makes things a little more fun and gives better opportunities for placing easter eggs.

Dialogue

The only dialogue I wrote was for the outdoors area and I put someone else on the task of writing and doing the dialogue for indoors and everywhere else, but I am unhappy with the result. I provided the font and the color of the box to be used for the dialogue but: 1. The dialogue box is black, 2. The font sizes are off, 3. The space between each line is too large, 4. The typewriter effect is missing, and 5. I don't really like the dialogue. So, I will be changing these things. I also drew all the sprites for the dialogue, including alternate poses and facial pieces for alternate expressions, but they weren't used, or weren't used the way I imagined. I will also make these changes. I also want to add vocals for the characters, but with sounds not actual voices.

Flour

The rush hour puzzle funtions, but there are improvements that I want to make. 1. The grid is very obvious, I think it should be of lower opacity or be removed completely, 2. The colors and sprites don't fit in with the rest of the game in terms of aesthetic, (This takes palce inside a bakery and the player is suddenly met with boxes) so I will redesign them and the background, and 3. I want to remove the limitation of moves. I plan to scrap this minigame and to redo it from scratch.

Eggs

Even though the catching game is the easiest of them all to create, it still took the person in charge 7 weeks to complete and test, and the quality still wasn't great. I made a lot of sprites to allow the player to customize their character to look the way they choose throughout the game. But, the person in charge of this minigame said implementing it was "too much," when I provided everything to make it possible. So the things that need to change here are 1. Including the customization made by the player on the main character, 2. Changing the background, which was one I provided but was not supposed to be used for this purpose, and 3. Changing everything to fit the aeshtetic of the game and to complement with my vision. I plan to scrap this minigame altogether and to redo it from scratch.

Milk

The pipe puzzle works, but there are improvements I would like to make. 1. Once again, the grid is very present, so I think it can be reduced or removed completely, 2. The colors and sprites once again don't fit with the aesthetic of the game and my vision for it, so I will redesign the sprites and add a new background, 3. I don't think there needs to be as many pieces as there are on the board, and I want to make the connections make more sense. I plan to scrap this minigame altogether and to redo it from scratch.

Sugar

Even though it functions, this wordsearch puzzle doesn't fit with the rest of the game. 1. The letters don't match the font I provided, 2. The background is a singular color, and is too saturated, 3. I might change the game for this ingredient simply because I can't think of a way to fit a wordsearch into the game although it originally didn't matter as much. I plan to scrap this minigame altogether and to redo it from scratch.

Butter

I was in charge of creating these puzzles and I am happy with the results because I know I could create what I imagined. However, I do want to add original music to this and all other minigames that I plan to commission from musicians on Fiverr. I also plan on adding sound effects to every minigame, which wasn't previously added.

Frosting

Originally this simon says game was known to be representing flavoring an icing parts of making a cake, but when I think about it more, it doesn't fit very well into the full game. 1. I think the art is super cool but it doesn't fit the pastel color palette of the game, 2. In this minigame the player is opening a locked box to get the ingredient to be used, but I don't remember seeing an explanation for why its locked up in the dialogue in the first place. I think I'm going to scrap and redesign the game for this ingredient, changing it to frosting, and changing it to a connect the dots kind of game with some obstacles between the paths.

Decoration

This game for this step is supposed to be series of mazes, but they are all very very basic in terms of design, sprites, and colors. 1. I will have to redesign the mazes, perhaps even change it into a running game with 3 lanes, 2. Change the colors, and 3. Add various sprites.

Miscellaneous

The ending was never dealt with by anyone in the team including myself, so I will implement it myself. I will also add end credits and elements of that sort. There was supposed to be a saving system. I created a continue button in the main menu, but it does't currently serve any function and there is no way to make a save or any automatic saving.
